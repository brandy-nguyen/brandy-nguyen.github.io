Brief Introduction

Hello, my name is Brandy Nguyen and I am the creator of this website. When I was originally introduced to the Hypertext Markup Language, I thought nothing of it other than being used to create plain text documents that can be read on browsers. My younger self was oblivious to how web pages are not only structured but they are also enhanced by deliberate styles wielded by the developers. In the core aspect of HTML, the structures are just rectangles and boxes with information placed within them. It sounds simple and it actually is that simple. The magic that makes websites lively and eye-popping is in how the developer uses Cascading Style Sheets to the best of their artistic vision.

Creating Websites From Scratch

During my time in class, I've learned to create HTML documents using a basic text editor like Notepad while learning about HTML standard practices. I am able to comprehend and explain the structure of my own website to you all since I have poured my heart and soul into it. Anyone can copy and paste code but it takes knowledge of one's own craft to be able to create new webpages. I strive to meet World Wide Web Consortium's goals of making the Web as accessible as possible for everyone. In doing so requires myself to abide by the standards set in place by the W3C to make sure that my own website renders in as much quality as possible.

Semantic Use Of Format & Styles

I mentioned in my introduction about how anyone can have the exact same content in their webpage but their styles make them unique from others. I highly recommend checking out the CSS Zen Garden to see CSS being used to its full potential across multiple styles. Presentation matters as much as content made for a target audience. However, web design is not simply placing glamorous colors to look pretty. Instead, it has a semantic purpose in making the user experience enhanced and usable. Notice how I placed my navigation structures near the top left so that users can spot them easily. You're most likely able to correctly determine the destinations of the navigation icons from their symbols. Even my color scheme is made to have a high contrast for people to see the content well. Every style element here on my website is a choice made deliberately.

Advocating For Accessibility

I aim to make my website to be perceivable by people, including those who are affected by blindness or color blindness. I am also aware that the user's viewing experience varies based on their connection speeds, type of device, screen resolution, web browser, and their need for accessibility. In fact, I desire to learn how to make my website more accessible because I believe that everyone has the right to access knowledge shared across the Internet. Once again, I emphasize the importance of following the standards of the W3C as they change to ensure that me and my sites are up to date with modern conventions. I know that as technology evolves, we must adapt but we should still lend our support to those unable to keep up or risk losing them.
